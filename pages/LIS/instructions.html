Instructions for hosts, volunteers, and other staff


  > Double-check any necessary materials, such as handouts, presentation slides, or promotional materials.
  > Test all gaming equipment and technology before the event to ensure everything is working as expected.
  > Set up the venue according to the event schedule and make sure all materials are ready to be used.
  > Be available and willing to greet attendees, answer questions, and direct them to appropriate areas as per their needs.
  > Monitor the event to ensure everything is running smoothly and make adjustments as needed.
  > Should there be any conflicts between/including event participants, follow the pertinent inclusivity/accessibility protocols listed
  > Clean up the venue after the event and return all equipment and other items to their proper locations.
  > Collect feedback from attendees and staff to identify ways to improve inclusivity and other factors for future events.


Inclusivity/Accessibiliy Protocols


  > ANY rules listed in the code of conduct are equally applicable to staff
  > Ensure that you know where accessible parking, ramps or lifts, and accessible bathrooms are located
  > Offer accommodations such as sign language interpreters, assistive listening devices, large print materials, or Braille materials to those with those needs.
  > Direct people with food allergies, sensitivities, or dietary restrictions appropriate dietary options
  > Direct people with mobility issues to seating options that can accommodate them.
  > Use gender-neutral language to make sure all attendees feel welcome regardless of gender, sexual orientation, or gender identity.
  > Make sure that attendees with disabilities are aware of the accessibility options and accommodations available.
  > In the case of a conflict, prioritize separating the people involved and de-escalating the conflict.
    - Call for immediate help in the case that there are any injuries or higher security/police is necessary to break up the fight.
    - Follow up on the participants before addressing their needs.
